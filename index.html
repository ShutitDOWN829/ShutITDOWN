What We're Talking About

How Did We Get Here?

Six things the industry hopes you never think about too carefully.

📈

How It Started

Subscription explicit content platforms launched as "creator economy" tools framed around empowerment and independence. They quietly became a multi-billion dollar industry with hundreds of millions of users — with almost no public accountability or regulatory framework.

💰

The Business Model

Platforms collect 20–25% of all creator earnings, then layer subscriptions, tips, pay-per-view unlocks, custom requests, and bundle promotions — all engineered to maximize per-user spending. These monetization mechanics mirror casino floor design, not creator tools.

🧠

Mental Health Crisis

Peer-reviewed research links compulsive use of explicit content platforms to depression, anxiety, sexual dysfunction, and impaired ability to connect with real partners. The WHO added compulsive sexual behavior disorder to ICD-11. Therapists report surging caseloads tied directly to these platforms.

💔

Relationship Damage

Multiple studies show consistent negative associations between explicit platform use and relationship satisfaction, trust, and commitment. Couples therapists across the country report it as one of the top presenting issues they see. The industry profits from damaged relationships.

🚸

Youth Exposure

The most common age verification mechanism on these platforms is a birthdate dropdown field. Independent researchers and child safety organizations have repeatedly documented minors accessing content with no friction. A dropdown is not a gate. It never was.

🔕

Why People Don't Know

The industry operates in a legal gray zone protected by broad platform liability shields. Mainstream media treats it as a third-rail topic. Advertisers don't ask. Banks look away. And users are too ashamed to talk about it — which is exactly the environment the industry depends on.

1 Corinthians 6:18–20

"Flee from sexual immorality. Every other sin a person commits is outside the body, but the sexually immoral person sins against his own body. "But I say to you that everyone who looks at a woman with lustful intent has already committed adultery with her in his heart."

"I have made a covenant with my eyes; how then could I gaze at a virgin?"

Job speaks of a deliberate, pre-committed guard over the gaze. In an era when these platforms put algorithmically-optimized content in front of every pair of eyes, this covenant language is more relevant than ever.

Proverbs 6:25–26

"Do not desire her beauty in your heart, and do not let her capture you with her eyelashes, for the price of a prostitute is only a loaf of bread, but a married woman hunts down a precious life."

"Let us behave properly as in the day, not in carousing and drunkenness, not in sexual promiscuity and sensuality, not in strife and jealousy. But put on the Lord Jesus Christ, and make no provision for the flesh in regard to its lusts."
